This inequality does not exist

Generative models are currently a hot topic in the startup world. They are used to produce artistic outputs. Could they also be used to visualise something about the world that is otherwise difficult to see?

This inequality does not exist is is a website like other websites in the refrain of This X does not exist. It is based on a generative model that creates fake images depicting the streets of London, based on a dataset from Google Streetview.

How it works

What is unique about this site is that the generation of images is guided by data about which parts of London suffer from deprivation of income, health or education. By pulling on the sliders next to images, you can compare the visual differences between privileged or deprived areas of the capital. It is a way to visualise an abstract social process, inequality in the city, that would otherwise be difficult to see.

The image generation is based on Generative Adverserial Networks (GANs). A technical paper describing the project is available here.

This inequality doesn't exist is an experiment in trying out how models such as GANs can be used to produce knowledge. Walking through the "latent space" of a GAN suggest a speculative configuration for knowledge production that relies on synthetic data and retains a role of interpretation by human participants. This process suggest that generative models can not just create new media, but visualise and made tangible abstract social processes.
